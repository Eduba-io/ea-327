02 · The frame we would bring

Most early-stage AI work does not belong in an AI layer.

About sixty percent is traditional code and database work. About thirty percent is rule-based logic. Roughly ten percent is a real AI problem.

For a BC founder entering a US regulated vertical, the same shape applies to the governance stack. BC companies arrive with PIPEDA-adequate privacy posture and a forward view on AIDA. The US counterpart requires state-by-state AI law, NIST AI RMF, sector-specific federal rules (HIPAA, FDA, SEC, NYDFS), and, for the defense-innovation corridor opened by the March PacifiCan announcement, ITAR, EAR, CMMC, and AI-specific DoD guidance on autonomous and decision-support systems.

03 · The relevant case

VigilOre. 160+ hours of compliance, compressed to under five minutes.

Eduba delivered a regulated-industry compliance engagement for VigilOre, a multi-agent platform for Democratic Republic of Congo mining compliance. Compliance work that previously took more than one hundred sixty hours compressed to under five minutes per case. The transferable piece is not the mining vertical. It is the methodology for decomposing compliance work into the layers that belong to traditional code, to rule-based logic, and to genuine AI, and for documenting the choice so an outside auditor or US government counterpart can read it.

At enablement scale. Eduba delivered training through Correlation One to Pacific Life and Colgate-Palmolive. 1,500+ people trained since May 2025. 6,000 to 9,000 hours saved per year. 95% of participants still using the tools thirty days after the workshop. KPMG UK, one of the Big Four, ran a forty-plus-executive track with the same team.
